# Anti-Patterns

These are patterns Garicano’s reasoning explicitly pushes against. Use them as warnings when critiquing proposals or advice.

## Confusing task automation with job automation

**Why it fails:** It ignores the full bundle of responsibilities, relationships, judgment, and tacit knowledge that labor markets actually price.

> automating parts of jobs uh tasks is not automating the job.

(sources: src_018, src_006, src_042, src_043)

## Predicting an instant AI unemployment apocalypse

**Why it fails:** It ignores unaffected sectors, elastic-demand expansion, messy residual tasks, new work, and complementarities between AI and workers.

> Yes, it's going to be a bigger revolution than what we've seen in the last 50 or 60 years in terms of automation. It's more similar maybe to the industrial revolution. But no, I don't think it's going to cause widespread uh long-term unemployment.

(sources: src_018)

## Ignoring the junior-training collapse risk

**Why it fails:** Even if senior roles survive, the pipeline can break when AI takes the menial work that paid for novice training.

> Across the economy, juniors pay for training by doing menial tasks. AI now performs an increasing share of that work, putting the bargain at risk.

(sources: src_037, src_038, src_040)

## Treating regulation as progress by default

**Why it fails:** Rules can burden European firms, reduce scale, suppress risk-taking, and impose costs without proportional benefits.

> But the truth is that we should simply think carefully through the arguments and judge them on the basis of cost-benefit analysis.

(sources: src_017, src_019, src_026)

## Claiming policy benefits are free

**Why it fails:** It hides distributional burdens, opportunity costs, and political backlash. Honest trade-offs are necessary for durable reform.

> What you cannot claim is that that's just for free.

(sources: src_019, src_026)

## Borrowing your way to growth without institutional discipline

**Why it fails:** Debt-financed spending can become politically expedient waste unless tied to public goods, reform, oversight, and incentives.

> The effectiveness of debt depends on how it is utilized. Borrowing to fund public expenditures without robust institutions leads to waste and erodes public trust.

(sources: src_012)

## Treating monetary policy as a substitute for reform

**Why it fails:** Central-bank intervention can calm crises but cannot solve low growth, debt sustainability, or missing fiscal and banking institutions.

> you cannot print your way uh out of trouble.

(sources: src_016)

## Misdiagnosing Europe’s startup problem as only a capital shortage

**Why it fails:** Capital can cross borders; the deeper bottleneck is often the shortage of investable companies, experienced founders, and ecosystem compounding.

> The problem is not supply of capital. It is supply of investable companies at the right stage.

(sources: src_009)

## Protecting incumbents and zombie firms from creative destruction

**Why it fails:** Innovation requires entry, exit, bankruptcy, and reallocation of people and capital from obsolete activities to new ideas.

> Market exit is not failure; it is how we reallocate assets, people, and resources from old businesses to new ideas.

(sources: src_026)

## Designing EU reforms that ignore scale and the Single Market

**Why it fails:** Technology and AI reward scale. Fragmented national rules prevent firms from growing into global competitors.

> The European Union does not need a new treaty or powers. It just needs a single-minded focus on one goal: economic prosperity.

(sources: src_026, src_014, src_016)

## Building a bank-protecting digital euro that users do not want

**Why it fails:** If the public option is unattractive, private stablecoins can win through network effects and weaken monetary sovereignty.

> Europe cannot have it both ways. It wants to protect its banks from competition while also defending the euro in the digital age. This won't work.

(sources: src_015)

## Relying on loose networks or self-regulation for systemic risk

**Why it fails:** Financial stability requires real authority, information, hierarchy, and enforcement power, especially across borders.

> the international system should substitute the current loose, networked structure for a more centralized and hierarchical one.

(sources: src_031)

## Weakening rule-of-law safeguards as procedural convenience

**Why it fails:** Broad-majority and judicial-independence safeguards are load-bearing institutional protections, not mere red tape.

> Creo que cambiar la ley para eliminar la necesidad de amplias mayorías para nombrar el órgano de gobierno del poder judicial marcará un antes y un después para España.

(sources: src_002)

## Appointing public managers without management competence

**Why it fails:** Complex ministries and supply chains require operational experience; competence is not decorative.

> ¿Por qué es tan difícil entender que alguien que no ha gestionado nunca nada ni puede dirigir un ministerio,ni decidir qué cadenas de suministro son esenciales?

(sources: src_002)
