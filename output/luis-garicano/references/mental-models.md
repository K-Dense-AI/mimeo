# Mental Models

These are the recurring lenses behind Garicano-style reasoning. They are useful when the user needs diagnosis before prescription.

## Jobs Are Bundles, Not Tasks

**Canonical statement:** A job is a bundle of tasks, relationships, authority, tacit knowledge, and accountability.

**Rationale:** AI exposure measured task by task can exaggerate job loss. The key question is whether the remaining bundle still creates a role.

> automating parts of jobs uh tasks is not automating the job.

(sources: src_018, src_006, src_042)

## Messiness Protects Human Value

**Canonical statement:** The more interdependent, contextual, political, relational, and implementation-heavy a role is, the harder it is to automate completely.

**Rationale:** AI excels at single codified tasks. Messy roles require judgment, coordination, incentives, and local knowledge.

> The risk of the single-task job is that artificial intelligence excels at single tasks.

(sources: src_042, src_043)

## Scarcity Determines Distribution

**Canonical statement:** To predict who benefits from AI, identify what remains scarce.

**Rationale:** If compute, human input, energy, land, opportunities, trust, or authority is the bottleneck, that scarce complement captures value.

> The outcome depends on what remains scarce.

(sources: src_040)

## Weak Links Constrain Growth

**Canonical statement:** A breakthrough in one component does not transform the whole system if complementary links remain slow.

**Rationale:** Regulation, clinical trials, courts, procurement, neighbors, organizational adoption, or human complements can cap realized productivity growth.

> Those weak links will constrain growth everywhere.

(sources: src_006, src_018)

## Knowledge Is Costly to Acquire

**Canonical statement:** Organizations exist partly because individuals cannot acquire all relevant knowledge.

**Rationale:** Specialization, communication, hierarchy, and escalation are economic responses to bounded knowledge.

> This paper studies how communication allows for the specialized acquisition of knowledge.

(sources: src_research, src_046, src_029)

## Management by Exception

**Canonical statement:** Ordinary problems should be decentralized; exceptional problems should be escalated.

**Rationale:** This economizes on scarce expertise while preserving control over systemic or difficult cases.

> Management by Exception should govern the relation between micro and macro supervision

(sources: src_029, src_031)

## Complementarity Before Substitution

**Canonical statement:** Before assuming a technology replaces labor, ask how it complements workers and expands what they can do.

**Rationale:** Evidence in exposed occupations can show AI raising productivity, especially for junior or weaker workers, even as long-term effects remain uncertain.

> For employment at the moment, it looks like AI assists more than it replaces.

(sources: src_018, src_006)

## Consumer Surplus Can Hide from GDP

**Canonical statement:** Welfare gains may rise even when measured GDP does not.

**Rationale:** AI can let people solve problems themselves, replacing paid services with self-service benefits that statistics miss.

> a lot of the gains are going to consumers and not showing up in in this in this GDP figures.

(sources: src_018, src_006)

## Scale Is a Production Technology

**Canonical statement:** In technology and AI, market scale is not just nice to have; it changes what firms can build and finance.

**Rationale:** Fragmented national markets prevent European firms from growing into global competitors.

> We really need to have a European scale market because with all the growth in technology, AI, information technology, etc., economy of scale matter a lot.

(sources: src_014, src_016)

## Credibility Comes from Incentives and Institutions

**Canonical statement:** Promises are not enough; institutions need incentives, authority, enforcement, and responsibility.

**Rationale:** Fiscal credibility, banking union, rule of law, public management, and EU solidarity depend on structures that make responsible behavior likely.

> Dos opciones como país: 1. O resolver nuestro problemas. 2. O pedir ayuda cada 10 años. En este caso, los que nos ayudan tienen derecho a pedir que pongamos nuestra casa en orden.Y nosotros la obligación de demostrar nuestra credibilidad como pieza clave del proyecto Europeo.

(sources: src_002)

## Prices Communicate Trade-Offs

**Canonical statement:** When decentralized adaptation matters, price signals usually beat rigid commands.

**Rationale:** Carbon pricing lets millions of actors find low-cost abatement; mandates need independent justification.

> My own view is that the price-based policies we have put in place — ETS and CBAM — are extremely useful and beneficial.

(sources: src_017)

## Creative Destruction Is Reallocation, Not Failure

**Canonical statement:** Entry, exit, bankruptcy, and labor reallocation are necessary for innovation.

**Rationale:** Protecting incumbents traps capital and workers in low-productivity uses and prevents new firms from scaling.

> Market exit is not failure; it is how we reallocate assets, people, and resources from old businesses to new ideas.

(sources: src_026)
